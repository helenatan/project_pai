# PM Adaptation Index — Community Strategy

---

## Phase 0 (before any code): Three posts, three evenings

These three actions establish first-mover credibility that cannot be replicated retroactively. Do them before writing a single line of code.

**Post 1: The Thesis Post**
Publish in your PM community. Frame the two narratives. Name the central question. Explain what you are building and why. No product to show -- just the question. Collect names for the email list in the comments.

**Post 2: The Hypothesis Poll**
Six yes/no/unsure polls, one per hypothesis, published in the community. Publish results immediately. These community priors are the "before" that makes the eventual findings compelling. "74% believed X. The data showed Y." That contrast is what gets shared.

**Post 3: The Methodology Note**
Before any data exists, publish how you are defining AI requirements, what the data cannot tell you, and what your confirmation criteria are. This is intellectual honesty published with a timestamp. It establishes the framework as yours.

---

## Weekly cadence (once live)

- **Every Saturday:** Cron runs. Digest generated. Email sent to subscribers.
- **Every Saturday in community:** Share digest with one qualitative prompt: "Does this match what you are seeing? Reply to tell us what the data cannot capture."
- **Month 2:** Publish first "State of the Signal" monthly recap (400 words). Share on LinkedIn.
- **Month 3:** First hypothesis reveal -- when H1 or H2 moves to a directional status, make it an event.

---

## Community email

Use Substack or Buttondown (both free). Set it up before launch. The email list is the most durable asset the project produces.

---

## Launch sequence

**Soft launch (Week 4):**
- Share dashboard with founding community members for feedback before public announcement
- Collect any UX issues or confusing elements; fix anything obvious

**Public launch (Weeks 5-6):**
- Publish launch essay (600-800 words): thesis, what the first weeks of data showed, what surprised you, what is still unresolved
- Post on LinkedIn
- Direct outreach to 5-10 PM newsletter writers with the data and an invitation to cite
- Announce to full community with "what do you see?" prompt

**Post-launch (Weeks 7-8):**
- Respond to every comment and reply in first two weeks
- Curate qualitative responses from community for weekly digest
- Write first monthly recap ("State of the Signal")

---

## Success signals to watch

- Community members sharing the digest unprompted
- Newsletter writers citing the data
- Qualitative responses that add texture the data cannot capture ("I'm seeing this on the ground too")
- Hypothesis poll results get referenced back when data starts to diverge from prior beliefs
